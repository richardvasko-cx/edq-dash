**User:** Target active Yahoo users only?

**Gemini:** Targeting only active Yahoo users is the correct immediate strategy to stabilize your domain reputation and alleviate the current deferral issues. When promotional frequency increases, mailbox providers like Yahoo prioritize reputation signals from highly engaged users; sending to inactive or low-engagement segments during high-frequency periods triggers negative signals that lead to throttling.

### Recommended Strategy
*   **Segment by Activity:** Restrict your upcoming sends to users who have opened or clicked within the last 30–60 days.
*   **Suppress Inactive Cohorts:** Temporarily exclude users who have not engaged in 90+ days. This reduces the "noise" that Yahoo’s filters use to classify your traffic as promotional spam.
*   **Normalize Cadence:** Return to your previous, lower-frequency cadence for at least 7–14 days to allow your reputation signals to recover.
*   **Gradual Re-introduction:** Once deferrals subside, slowly re-introduce less active segments in small, controlled batches rather than returning to the full, high-frequency volume immediately.

### Performance Context
Your current metrics show an **Accepted Rate of 95.9%** and a **Bounce Rate of 4.1%**, indicating that while you are experiencing deferrals, your fundamental authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) is healthy. The issue is purely reputation-based due to the volume spike.

**Next Step:** Use your segment builder to create a "Yahoo Active" audience (e.g., `Last Email Open` or `Last Email Click` within 30 days) and apply this as a mandatory filter for all upcoming campaigns targeting the `email.morrisons.com` domain until the deferral rate normalizes.
