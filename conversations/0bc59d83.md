Root Cause Analysis: Microsoft temporarily suspended the sending IP after sustained high throughput, creating a retry backlog and high subsequent-attempt latency.

**Gemini:** The root cause of the delivery delays for Sainsbury's (ticket 26060002) is a **temporary suspension of the sending IP by Microsoft**, triggered by sustained high-throughput sending that exceeded their filtering thresholds. This resulted in a significant retry backlog and increased latency for Nectar offer emails.

To resolve this and prevent future 451 4.7.650 deferral errors, you should implement the following strategy:

*   **Implement Custom Rate Limiting:** Configure Braze custom rate limiting specifically to align with Microsoft’s sustained throughput thresholds. This will throttle outgoing traffic to a level that Microsoft’s filters can process without triggering deferrals.
*   **Apply a Sunset Policy:** Suppress unengaged users (those with no opens or clicks in the last 180 days). This reduces the volume of low-value traffic, which is critical for improving your overall engagement signals and reducing reputation strain.
*   **Segment and Scale:** Prioritize highly engaged users for upcoming campaigns. Gradually scale your sending volume to allow Microsoft’s filters time to adjust to your traffic patterns.
*   **Monitor Reputation:** Use the Microsoft SNDS (Smart Network Data Services) dashboard to track your IP reputation status and verify that the retry backlog is clearing effectively.

**Next Step:** Review your current Braze campaign send settings and apply the recommended rate-limiting caps to ensure alignment with Microsoft’s throughput capacity.
