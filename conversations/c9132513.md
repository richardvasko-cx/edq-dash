**User:** Diagnose a Gmail bounce spike?

**Gemini:** A Gmail bounce spike is typically driven by either **reputation degradation** due to low engagement or **list hygiene issues** related to stale data. 

To diagnose the specific cause, cross-reference your bounce logs with the following patterns:

### 1. High Hard Bounce Rate (Invalid Recipient)
If the spike consists primarily of "Hard" bounces, the issue is likely **list hygiene**. 
*   **Cause:** Sending to unverified or "dormant" addresses that have been abandoned or deactivated by Gmail.
*   **Fix:** Implement a strict sunset policy. Exclude users who have not engaged with your emails in the last 90–180 days. 
*   **Precedent:** A peer account resolved a similar spike by scrubbing dormant segments that were triggering high invalid-recipient counts.

### 2. High Soft Bounce/Spam Folder Rate
If the bounce logs show deferred events or high volume landing in the Spam folder, the issue is **reputation/engagement**.
*   **Cause:** "Promotional over-mailing" or lack of sufficient suppression between overlapping campaigns. Gmail throttles or blocks senders when engagement rates (open/click) drop below their threshold for your domain.
*   **Fix:** 
    *   **Consolidate:** Reduce frequency and prioritize high-intent segments.
    *   **Suppression:** Ensure strict cross-campaign suppression so users do not receive multiple messages in a short window.
*   **Precedent:** A peer account resolved this by limiting campaign frequency and tightening audience suppression, which allowed their domain reputation to recover at Gmail.

### 3. Authentication Check
Before adjusting volume, verify that your technical foundation is sound. Use the **Tools > Google Dig** feature to confirm:
*   **SPF:** Ensure your record includes `include:amazonses.com` (or your specific Braze sending infrastructure).
*   **DKIM:** Confirm your selector is correctly published in your DNS.
*   **DMARC:** Verify you have a policy (preferably `p=none` for monitoring or `p=quarantine`/`reject` for protection) to prevent spoofing.

**Next Step:** Use the **Message Header Analyzer** tool on a sample of the bounced emails to check for specific Gmail error codes (e.g., `550-5.7.1`), which will explicitly state if the block is due to reputation (Spam) or list quality (Invalid).
