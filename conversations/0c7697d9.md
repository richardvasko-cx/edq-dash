**User:** Reduce Morrisons promotional volume?

**Gemini:** Yes, reducing promotional volume for Morrisons is the correct immediate action to address the current Yahoo deferral issue (Case 26060004).

The root cause of the current Yahoo deferrals is identified as the recent doubling of promotional frequency, which has diluted engagement and triggered throttling protections. While authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) is healthy and passing, the volume expansion has outpaced the established reputation signals required by Yahoo’s infrastructure.

**Data-Driven Rationale:**
*   **Volume Correlation:** Historical metrics show that peak volume days (e.g., May 26th with 54,965 targeted) correlate with sustained high deferral counts.
*   **Engagement Impact:** The current 15.1% open rate indicates a significant portion of the list is not actively engaging; sending to these users at an increased frequency is negatively impacting sender reputation and forcing mailbox providers to defer traffic to manage the load.
*   **Bounce Profile:** The 4.1% bounce rate, combined with the "temporary deferrals" classification in the root cause, confirms that Yahoo is actively throttling incoming traffic rather than blocking it permanently.

**Recommended Next Steps:**
1.  **Immediate Throttling:** Reduce daily send volumes to align with the pre-doubling levels (targeting the ~25k–30k range observed prior to the recent spikes).
2.  **Engagement-Based Suppression:** Exclude users who have not opened or clicked in the last 30–60 days. This will improve the sender-to-recipient engagement ratio, which is the primary signal Yahoo uses to determine throughput capacity.
3.  **Gradual Re-ramp:** Once Yahoo deferrals stabilize, slowly re-introduce segments of the lower-engagement audience, monitoring for any recurrence of the 4.1% bounce rate or elevated deferral signals.
