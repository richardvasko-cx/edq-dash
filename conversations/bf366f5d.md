**User:** Safe IP warming ramp rate?

**Gemini:** A safe IP warming ramp rate is defined by a **gradual, conservative increase in volume** that allows mailbox providers to recognize your sending patterns and build a positive reputation for your new IP address. There is no single "magic number," as the appropriate rate depends on your list quality, engagement levels, and the specific mailbox providers (e.g., Gmail, Microsoft, Yahoo) in your audience.

### Best Practices for a Safe Ramp
*   **Prioritize Engagement:** Only send to your most active, engaged users during the first 1–2 weeks of warming. High engagement signals to mailbox providers that your traffic is wanted.
*   **Start Small:** Begin with a low daily volume (e.g., 500–1,000 emails per day per IP) and increase volume by no more than 50–100% every 2–3 days, provided your bounce and complaint rates remain low.
*   **Monitor Thresholds:** 
    *   **Bounce Rate:** Keep below 2%.
    *   **Complaint Rate:** Keep below 0.1% (1 complaint per 1,000 emails).
*   **Consistency:** Send consistently every day. Erratic sending patterns (e.g., sending 100k one day and zero the next) can trigger spam filters.
*   **Pause if Necessary:** If you see a spike in bounces or complaints, pause your warming schedule immediately. Investigate the cause—such as a list quality issue or an authentication failure—before resuming at a lower volume.
